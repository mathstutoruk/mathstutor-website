Bio

Academic background, classroom experience and a carefully developed approach to teaching mathematics.

Surinder Sangha

Experience, academic record, teaching insight and a broader sense of the perspective brought into mathematics tuition. A mathematics tutor with a strong academic background, teacher training in Secondary Mathematics, and experience across classroom teaching and one-to-one tuition. The focus throughout is on clarity, careful structure and helping students develop genuine mathematical understanding.

Experience

Teacher training in Secondary Mathematics was undertaken at Brunel University, with experience gained across a range of inner-city and diverse school settings, including both state schools and independent colleges.

Strong classroom management and secure subject command were developed in demanding teaching environments, with lessons shaped to support participation, understanding and meaningful progress across a broad range of abilities.

Academic Record

A Bachelor of Science (BSc Hons) from Loughborough University, completed in 2016, provided a strong grounding in pure mathematics, logical rigour and formal mathematical argument.

Academic study also included A-levels in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics and Philosophy, supporting a broad analytical foundation across both conceptual and quantitative disciplines.

Academic Credentials

My academic background includes A-level studies in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics and Philosophy, providing a strong foundation across both analytical and conceptual disciplines. I later completed a BSc (Hons) in Mathematics at Loughborough University in 2016, with a particular focus on pure mathematics, logical reasoning and formal problem solving. AS-level Philosophy was completed one year early during GCSE studies, achieving 93% — the highest mark nationally.

Teaching Insight

Alongside subject study, mathematics education formed an important part of academic development. This provided insight into how students engage with mathematical ideas, where common misconceptions arise, and how difficult concepts can be made more accessible without sacrificing precision.

Approach

Lessons are built around clarity, structure and active engagement. Complex ideas are broken into manageable stages, while questioning and reflection are used to move students beyond memorisation and towards more secure mathematical reasoning.

Wider Development

Ongoing engagement with research and advanced ideas in mathematics education continues to shape the teaching approach. There is a particular interest in how mathematical thinking can be developed effectively, and how students can be guided towards deeper and more independent understanding.

Beyond the Classroom

Wider experiences, including travel and exposure to different environments, contribute to a broader perspective on communication, personal development and the importance of adaptability. These experiences also help shape a calm, thoughtful and human approach to working with students.

Teacher Training & Classroom Experience

I undertook teacher training in Secondary Mathematics at Brunel University, gaining experience across a range of inner-city and diverse school settings, including both state schools and independent colleges. During this time, I developed strong classroom management skills and a confident command of subject knowledge, enabling me to deliver engaging and structured lessons across varying abilities.

Mathematics Education & Teaching Insight

As part of my academic development, I also explored mathematics education, including how students engage with mathematical ideas, where common misconceptions arise, and how difficult concepts can be explained more clearly. This continues to shape the way I teach, helping students build confidence and deeper understanding rather than relying on memorisation alone.

Ongoing Study & Development

Alongside my academic background, I maintain an ongoing interest in mathematics education and regularly engage with research and advanced ideas around teaching methods. There is a particular focus on how mathematical thinking can be developed effectively and how students can be guided towards more independent understanding over time.

Employability Award & Practical Experience

Alongside my degree, I completed the Employability Award at Loughborough University, gaining hands-on experience working with students from disadvantaged backgrounds. This strengthened my ability to build rapport, adapt explanations to different learners, and create a focused and supportive learning environment.

Teaching Approach

My teaching approach combines clarity, structure and active engagement. Complex ideas are broken down into manageable steps, students are encouraged to ask questions, and lessons are shaped around genuine understanding rather than surface-level memorisation. The aim is not only improved grades, but stronger confidence and more independent mathematical thinking.

What Students Can Expect

Students can expect a supportive and focused learning environment, where progress is built carefully step by step. Whether the goal is stronger grades, better examination preparation, or more secure foundations, lessons are designed to provide clear guidance, consistent support and meaningful long-term progress.
