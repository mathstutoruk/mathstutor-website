Mathematics Lessons

Differentiation Basics
f(x) = 3x² + 4x − 7 f'(x) = 6x + 4 Power rule applied term by term Useful for algebraic fluency and early calculus confidence

Clear presentations across major topics help students build secure foundations before moving to more demanding problems.

Integration Practice
∫(6x + 4) dx = 3x² + 4x + c Reverse of differentiation Constant of integration included Supports method accuracy and fluency

Worked examples and structured notes encourage precision, helping students develop reliable methods across core calculus.

Trigonometric Reasoning
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 tanθ = sinθ / cosθ Exact values and identities reinforced Frequent source of A-Level marks

Lessons emphasise careful explanation of identities, exact values and transformations so that trigonometry becomes less mechanical and more intuitive.

Algebraic Manipulation
x² − 5x + 6 = (x − 2)(x − 3) 2/(x+1) + 1/(x−1) Common denominators and factorisation Essential for higher-level confidence

Strong algebra underpins success across the subject, so these slides focus on fluency, accuracy and flexibility with expressions.

Graphs and Functions
y = (x − 2)² + 1 Turning point at (2,1) Translations and transformations Links algebra to visual interpretation

Graph-based explanations help students visualise ideas clearly and connect algebraic method with geometric meaning.

Sequences and Series
Arithmetic: aₙ = a + (n−1)d Geometric: aₙ = arⁿ⁻¹ Sum formulae practised step by step Useful for exam consistency

Presentation materials break formula use into manageable steps, helping students avoid the small errors that often lose marks.

Exam Technique
Show full working clearly Define variables before substitution Use exact values where required Check units and signs

Examination technique is addressed directly, with practical habits that improve mark collection and reduce avoidable mistakes.

Problem Solving
Interpret the question first Break the task into smaller steps Choose the most efficient method Review whether the answer is sensible

More demanding questions are used to build resilience, independent thinking and the confidence to handle unfamiliar problems.
